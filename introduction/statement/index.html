Who We Are

Cornerstone Christian Academy (CCA) is registered with the Maryland State Department of Education as a non-profit church-exempt school. CCA is a member of the Association of Christian Schools International (ACSI).

As a ministry of Cornerstone Church, our mission is four-fold. We are building an authentic community, lifting up Jesus, growing in Him and touching our world. We believe that the family, church and school form a triangle of influence in a child’s life. The school can never compensate for the home. Therefore, we form a partnership with our parents to build a strong foundation of faith.

We desire to equip our students to grow in their love for God and desire to obey His Word. We want to teach them to respect God, respect their leaders, and respect each other. Our approach will be one of Redemptive Discipline. Our goal is for students to not only learn to obey out of love for Christ but most importantly be conformed into the image of Christ (Romans 8:29). We desire that student learn to take ownership for their choices and ultimately learn what God’s expectations are for them.

We will focus on character traits taught throughout all grades and help deepen the students understanding and meaning behind each character trait. Developing good inward character traits will ultimately show in a student’s outward actions.

In order to cultivate a positive social and educational atmosphere, students will learn to recognize that acceptable standards of behavior are important. Redemptive discipline reminds us that our ability to do what is right depends on a daily renewing of the heart as well as allowing the Holy Spirit and Word to grow within us. Discipline will be administered when any individual’s actions interfere with the right of the teachers to teach and the students to learn.

In order to see young lives molded, we strive to understand individuals and recognize each may be at a different level of maturity. We also seek to distinguish between those who have made first-time mistakes and those who are repeat offenders or mockers, although in some circumstances, first-time offenses may be of sufficient severity to warrant suspension or expulsion.

Where appropriate, we want to encourage students to grow and change as a result of their mistakes and failures. This is not to say there will not be consequences for these actions, but rather, we want to allow students to appropriately experience consequences and the growth that can result from them.



Statement of Purpose


Discipleship:
CCA provides a Christ centered education where the Word of God is an integral part of the entire curriculum. Our staff is committed to seeing each student achieve their fullest potential both academically and spiritually. Our students will acquire wisdom, knowledge and a biblical worldview as evidenced by a lifestyle of character, service, stewardship, worship and leadership.

Prayer:
CCA earnestly desires to have students participate in times of prayer and to encourage a culture of students dedicated to prayer. Students have opportunities to pray in chapel, in the classroom, at extracurricular activities and will learn that prayer is an important part of their walk with the Lord.

Worship Experience:
We want to present Christ and His Word in a genuine and powerful way through every chapel service and activity.

Leadership Development:
The staff at CCA is committed to developing and nurturing students as future servant leaders in their church and in their community. Technology: Academically, technology is an integral part of the education process and CCA is committed to utilizing technology to enhance the learning experience.


Our Beliefs & Guiding Philospohy


Cornerstone holds to an orthodox historical Christian faith, as reflected in these basic statements of our beliefs. About the Bible: We believe that the Bible is fully inspired by God, that it is entirely trustworthy and is our guide for faith and life. (2 Timothy 3:16)

About God:
We believe that God is the loving Creator of all that exists, both seen and unseen. God is eternal and completely good, knowing all things, having all power and majesty. God exists eternally as the Trinity, in three persons, yet one God: Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. (Isaiah 40:28, Genesis 1:1-31)

About Jesus:
We believe that God expressed Himself in human form through Jesus Christ. Jesus was born of the Virgin Mary and lived a sinless life. We believe He was crucified for the sins of us all, rose again from the dead and ascended to heaven. (John 11:25-26)

About the Holy Spirit:
We believe that the Holy Spirit is sent from God to live in all who believe in Jesus. He teaches, comforts, and empowers us, giving each follower of Jesus diverse gifts for serving in the church and serving others in the world. We believe it is through the Holy Spirit that we change and develop a holy life and Jesus-like character. (Acts 1:8)

About Salvation:
We believe that all people are created with dignity and great value in the image of God and that people were created to live in a thriving relationship with God. However, through our sin (failing to live by God’s moral standards), we break our intended relationship with God and we experience the sad consequences of that broken relationship, both spiritually and socially. However, because of God’s love for us, He sent His Son, Jesus, to rescue us from those consequences and to restore our broken relationship with God. Jesus´ death on the cross was a perfect act of redemption for each of us. We receive the free gift of forgiveness and are spiritually reborn through repenting (changing our mind and heart) of our sin and placing faith in Jesus alone. (John 3:16)

About the Church:
We believe that the church is comprised of any person, anywhere in the world who puts their faith in Jesus. The church itself is people who are empowered by God’s Spirit to be part of the mission of Jesus here on earth. The church is one global community, but has smaller, local expressions, such as Cornerstone. Each local church expression has a unique personality and we see beauty in that diversity. We believe that the church does not exist for itself, but exists as a community of worshipers who are here to serve others, as we bring the love and hope of Jesus to the world around us. (Ephesians 2:19-20)

About the Afterlife and End Times:
We believe there is a literal heaven and hell and one day Jesus will return. We approach the topics of the afterlife and end times with great mystery and wonder. (Colossians 3:1-4)

Divine Healing:
Wherever Jesus went, He ministered with compassion and healed the sick. Christ’s example showed an interconnection with salvation because many believed following their healing. Our Lord continues to heal today and it is vital that the church preach, teach and practice this biblical truth. Scriptures command believers to pray in faith and trust God for the outcome. (Matthew 4:23, 1 Peter 2:24)

Empowerment of the Holy Spirit:
The doctrine of the Baptism in the Holy Spirit is our distinctive. This truth explains the passion and power of our witness. Jesus promised His followers they would receive power from on high so that they would be His witnesses. We believe this truth is just as relevant today as our Fellowship continues to take the gospel message around the globe. (Acts 2:38)

The Second Coming:
With the world experiencing natural disasters, economic downturns, and increasing uncertainties on many fronts, the doctrine of Christ’s Second Coming is more relevant than ever. Believers must rest in the certainty of the imminent return of our Lord, live their lives in view of this, and share this hope with others. (2 Peter 3:11-13)

Marriage and Family:
We believe God defines marriage as the permanent, exclusive, comprehensive, and conjugal “one flesh” union of one man and one woman, intrinsically ordered to procreation and biological family. (Genesis 1:27–28; 2:18–24; Mark 10:5–9). We believe that God has commanded that no intimate sexual activity be engaged in outside of a marriage between a man and a woman. We believe that any form of homosexuality, lesbianism, bisexuality, transgenderism, adultery, and pornography are sinful perversions of God's gift. (Genesis 2:24; Lev. 18:1-30; Romans 1:26-29; I Corinthians 5:1, 6:9; I Thessalonians 4:1-8; Hebrews 13:4)
